What happened after Facebook COO Sheryl Sandberg praised Indian     teen's app?
Back



A direct and immediate outcome of being lauded by a well known personality is that everyone in the world wants to befriend you.
Last week, 19-year-old Harsh Songra was one such recipient of a mountain of friend requests right after Facebook's COO Sheryl Sandberg wrote about him, posting, "We're supporting developers like Harsh who have great ideas but can't always access the resources they need." Sandberg specially mentioned Bhopal-based Songra for his Android app called My Child, which can predict if a child is likely to suffer from a developmental disorder in 45 seconds. The apps calculations are made by working with basic parameters like a child's height, weight and gender.
Songra, whose app launch almost coincided with his selection to Facebook's FB Start program in January this year, feels Sandberg may have glowingly referred to his app because of the idea and personal story behind it. At the age of 11, he was diagnosed with dyspraxia but it took nine long years for Songra's parents to find out he has this particular form of developmental coordination disorder.
